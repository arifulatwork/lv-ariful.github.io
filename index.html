General Application Process for New Students

  1. Accept Conditional Offer: Confirm acceptance of your offer letter from the university.
  2. Submit Application Fee: Pay the application fee (ranging from ‚Ç¨150 to ‚Ç¨300).
  3. Interview/Test: Attend and succeed in the required interview or test.
  4. Document Submission: Be prepared to submit additional documents for AIC (Academic Information Centre) verification as requested by the university.
  5. AIC Report: Wait for the AIC report, which typically takes about 2 months (can extend to 4 months in some cases).
  6. Admission Letter: If the AIC report is positive, you'll receive an admission letter. Pay the tuition and deposit fees:
    • Tuition fees: ‚Ç¨2,900 to ‚Ç¨4,500
    • Deposit: ‚Ç¨500 for public universities (may equal tuition fees for private universities).
  7. Study Agreement: After payment, submit the receipt to receive a study agreement form. Read this carefully as it contains important information.
  8. Invitation Number: Upon signing the study agreement and sending it back, the university will obtain an invitation number from the immigration department (2-3 weeks).
  9. Residence Permit Application: Use the invitation number to apply online for a residence permit appointment at the Latvian Embassy in your country.
  10. Visa Application: After the appointment, apply for an Indian Double Entry Visa.

Note on PhD Applications

The process may vary, as PhD candidates often need to submit a research proposal first.

Cost Breakdown for Studying in Latvia (Before Visa)

  • 1. Application Fee: You must first apply to your chosen university, which typically costs around 150 euros or more. This fee includes the cost of AIC (Academic Information Centre) verification.
    • Approximate cost: 150 euros (around 26,000 BDT)
  • 2. Tuition Fee & Deposit Fee (After AIC Verification): After receiving a positive AIC result, you need to pay your tuition fee and deposit fee.
    • Tuition Fees: Range from 2500 to 4500 euros, depending on the university.
    • Deposit Fee: 500 euros for government universities (this amount may vary for private universities).
    • Approximate tuition cost: 2500-4500 euros, Deposit: 500 euros
  • 3. Invitation Letter & Embassy Appointment: Once you receive your invitation letter, you'll need to book an appointment at the Latvian Embassy in India. This service is free of charge.
  • 4. Indian Visa Application: After securing your appointment, you must apply for a double-entry Indian visa.
    • Cost: About 824 BDT
  • 5. Travel to India: You need to travel to Delhi, India, for your embassy appointment. The cost for this travel is based on your personal preference and travel arrangements.
  • 6. Police Clearance Legalization: You must legalize your police clearance certificate at the Bangladeshi High Commission in India, which costs Rs 180 (payable via card or bank deposit).
  • 7. Latvian Embassy Fees in India: At the Latvian Embassy, after your interview, you will need to make the following payments:
    • Consular Fee: Around 80 euros
    • State Fees (for processing your visa):
      • 560 euros for 5-day processing
      • 280 euros for 10-day processing
      • 160 euros for 1-month processing
    • Courier Fee: 40 euros to send your documents to Latvia
    • Apostille Fee for Police Clearance: 25 euros
    • For bachelor's students, your SSC and HSC/Diploma transcripts and certificates must be apostilled. The fee is ‚Ç¨25 per document. If you have two certificates (SSC and HSC) and two transcripts (SSC and HSC), the total cost will be ‚Ç¨100.
    • For master's and PhD applicants, your SSC, HSC/Diploma, and undergraduate certificates and transcripts must be legalized. For PhD applicants, your master's certificate and transcript must also be apostilled. The fee is ‚Ç¨25 per document.

Note: Be cautious of agencies that might overcharge you. There are no additional costs involved before the visa application beyond what is mentioned.

You can arrange your bank statements in two ways: Parental Bank Statement or Personal Bank Statement

1. Parental Bank Statement:
You can provide the original bank statement from one of your parents showing finances of not less than 17,800 EUR. Along with this, you must submit:

  • A legalized birth certificate or NID from Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Bangladesh
  • A notarially approved sponsorship letter

2. Personal Bank Statement:
You can open a bank account with funds of not less than 8,900 EUR and submit the original bank statement, stamped and signed by your bank.

  • A legalized birth certificate or NID from Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Bangladesh
  • A notarially approved sponsorship letter

In Latvia, the acceptable study gap varies by program level. For bachelor's programs, a gap of up to 5 years is generally acceptable. For master's programs, a gap of up to 10 years is typically accepted. However, you must provide a valid justification for the gap. This often involves submitting a justification letter explaining the reasons for the delay in your studies.

Once you have the verified certificate, take it to the Bangladesh High Commission New Delhi, India, At the high commission, you'll need to pay a fee of 180 INR via card, so ensure you also bring a photocopy of the police clearance certificate. It's important to visit the embassy during weekdays, specifically Monday to Friday, between 11:00 AM and 12:30 PM. After submission, the legalization process typically takes 1 to 2 days to complete.

After receiving a positive OCMA decision for your Latvian visa, the next steps are as follows:

1. Schedule an Appointment:

You need to schedule an appointment with VFS Latvia to submit your passport and other required documents. You can do this by either:

  • Sending an email to info.latviain@vfshelpline.com
  • Calling VFS Latvia (India) at +91 22 67866025 (08:00 to 17:00, Monday to Friday)

2. Submit Your Documents:

On the appointment day, submit your passport and other required documents at the VFS center for further processing.

3. Additional Information:

For detailed instructions and updates, you can visit the official Latvian Ministry of Foreign Affairs website here.

Make sure to follow these steps promptly to avoid delays in the visa issuance process.
